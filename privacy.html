Privacy Policy

Effective: July 16, 2026

CrossPost Studio is a Windows desktop application. It does not operate a public media library and does not sell personal data.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy explains how CrossPost Studio handles information when a creator connects an account and publishes a video to YouTube, Facebook, Instagram or TikTok. CrossPost Studio is an independent application and is not endorsed by those platforms.

2. Information stored on the Windows device

The application stores connected-account identifiers, creator-selected account labels, publishing preferences and OAuth tokens needed to maintain authorized connections. Secrets and tokens are encrypted using Windows secure storage when the operating system supports it. Local settings remain on the device until the creator removes an account, clears application data or uninstalls the application.

3. Information transferred to social platforms

When the creator presses Publish, CrossPost Studio transfers the selected video and creator-entered metadata only to the selected destination platforms. Depending on the platform, this may include a title or caption, description, hashtags, visibility, audience classification, category and interaction settings.

CrossPost Studio also uses official platform APIs to obtain the connected account name, available publishing options, authorization status and processing result. Each platform processes this information under its own privacy policy.

4. No automatic publishing

CrossPost Studio does not publish in the background without a creator action. The creator must select a local file, choose one or more authorized destinations, review available settings and start the publishing operation.

5. External services

The application communicates with official services operated by Google/YouTube, Meta/Facebook/Instagram and TikTok. Browser-based authorization pages are controlled by those providers. GitHub Pages hosts this informational website and may process ordinary web-server data such as IP address, request time and browser information under GitHub’s privacy practices.

6. Sharing and sale of personal data

CrossPost Studio does not sell personal information. Information is transferred only as needed to provide creator-requested publishing functions, comply with law, protect security or respond to a valid legal request.

7. Data retention and deletion

Local account data remains until the creator removes the connection or clears the application data. To disconnect an account, open Connected Accounts in CrossPost Studio and remove the account. Creators may also revoke access from the security or connected-app settings of the relevant social platform.

For assistance with deletion or privacy questions, use the CrossPost Studio Support page.

8. Security

CrossPost Studio uses operating-system encryption for stored secrets, HTTPS for platform communication, OAuth state validation and PKCE where required. No system can guarantee absolute security. Creators should protect their Windows account and revoke a connection if they believe it has been compromised.

9. Children

CrossPost Studio is intended for creators who are legally permitted to operate the connected social accounts. It is not directed to children under 13, and platform-specific age requirements continue to apply.

10. Changes

This policy may be updated when the application, platform integrations or legal requirements change. The effective date at the top identifies the latest published version.

11. Contact

Questions, privacy requests and account-deletion requests can be submitted through the public channels on the Support page.
